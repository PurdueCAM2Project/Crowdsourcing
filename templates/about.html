About Us

CAM2 Crowdsourcing Team was created on Jan 8th 2019 under the Purdue CAM2 Research Group. The purpose of this team is to explore the utilization of Crowdsourcing to detect and report the biased attributes in single-object image datasets. We have developed a website of Human Intelligence Tasks(HIT) and started collecting crowdsourcing data on Amazon Mechanical Turk(AMT). Potential focuses of this team can be any topics related to human interactions or collecting human crowd data. Crowdsourcing utilizes the knowledge of humans to complete a task. In the scope of research, we are using the crowd to handle specific tasks that may be hard for a machine to do or improve the work of a machine. This may include tasks such as detecting bias within image datasets using human knowledge rather than machines, since humans are better at distinguishing features within images. We use the crowd to classify images to datasets and generate a confusion matrix detailing the similarity of images across several datasets.
