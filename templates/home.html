What is dataset bias?

Dataset is a collection of related sets of information that is composed of separate elements but can be manipulated as a unit by a computer. A dataset's structure and properties are defined by several characteristic. When a dataset is not representative of the population or phenomenon of study, it's "biased".

Why is bias important?

Computer vision technologies have been applied to an increasingly wide range of applications from autonomous navigation, to medical image analysis, to precision agriculture. Despite many exciting innovations in computer vision, recent studies reveal a number of risks in existing computer vision systems, suggesting results of such systems may be unfair and untrustworthy.

Being able to detect potential sampling biases in the visual dataset prior to model development is thus essential for mitigating the fairness and trustworthy concerns in computer vision.
